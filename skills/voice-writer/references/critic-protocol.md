# Critic Protocol

Run this review process on any output before presenting it.

## Process

1. Review the full output against references/voice-profile.md and references/banned-phrases.md.
2. Verify all Constraints from SKILL.md are satisfied.
3. Assign a rating: Needs Work, Good, or Excellent.
4. If below Excellent, provide specific feedback referencing the exact rules violated.
5. Revise the output based on feedback.
6. Review the revised version.
7. Repeat until Excellent or 3 rounds complete (whichever comes first).

## What to Check

### Voice Match
- Does this read like the voice profile? Same rhythm, sentence length, formality level?
- Are any banned phrases present?
- Does it use words and phrases Jack actually uses?
- Would someone who works with Jack recognize his voice?

### Substance
- Does the output actually answer what was asked?
- Are claims specific and grounded with concrete details?
- Is anything padded or restated in different words?
- Is the length appropriate for the content type? (Messages: 80-200 words. Proposals: calibrate to purpose.)

### Final Bar
- Would Jack send, publish, or present this without editing?
- If not, what specifically needs to change?

## Rules for the Critic
- Be specific. "The tone is off" is useless. "Paragraph 3 uses 'Furthermore' which is banned, and the sentence structure is more formal than Jack's typical writing" is useful.
- Critique from Jack's standards, not general writing standards.
- Don't over-polish. Jack's voice includes technical directness and measured confidence. That's a feature.
- 3 rounds max. After 3, present the best version with a note on anything still flagged.
